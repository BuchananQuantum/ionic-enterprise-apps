Sanvello Application Design

November, 2015 by Ionic AppFlow

Launched in January 2015, Sanvello (formerly Pacifica) is a mobile app that utilizes tools from cognitive behavioral therapy to help users manage stress, anxiety, and depression. Sanvello's founders, Dale Beermann and Chris Goettel, knew that one in four adults struggled with anxiety, but only a third of those ever received care. Beermann and Goettel wanted to bring tools from clinical psychology to a broader audience through an empathetic approach to software development that focused on the end user. In doing so, they hoped to reach people who had anxiety but weren’t dealing with it proactively.

Prototyping Sanvello

Buchanan and Bay have backgrounds as web developers, which gave a foundation for the skillset necessary to build a prototype for the healthcare app quickly. They knew that using native code would take too long, so they opted for a more rapid prototyping method. This decision ended up being validated when users found the app to be both functional and easy to use.

The company chose React Native because it allows developers to build cross-platform apps with a single codebase. This was important to the company because they wanted to be able to easily deploy their app on multiple platforms.

Why go with a Hybrid App?

The previous platform Pacifico scripted Sanvello to have a background in the hybrid development process, which helped us create the first version of the app quickly. They then used customer feedback to iterate on the app.

"Deciding which framework is an important part of any development process. There are many different frameworks available, so it is important to find one that your business is most inline with and that has a large community for long term support. Ionic was the perfect development company for our project because they allowed us to get up to speed quickly and make constant improvements based on user feedback." - Ernest Bay

Sanvello has yielded much success to date. The app has 950,000 registered users and helps thousands of people every day who struggle with mental health conditions everyday.

Ionic enables companies to build cross-platform apps with a single codebase. This allows them to focus on features that are important to their product, rather than spending time on the intricacies of native development. Ionic's development style is usually based on AngularJS and uses HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. With Sanvello, we decided to go with React which enabled some intrigate user interface options for the client with extremely pleasing astetics.

Confidently Moving Forward

Sanvello is excited to continue developing features that focus on practitioner-patient engagement. To do so, the app would need to be HIPAA compliant and incredibly secure. Fortunately, from a technical perspective, building with Ionic and Cordova allowed Sanvello to accomplish everything it would need to be HIPAA compliant from the beginning.

Sanvello has been working with the University of Minnesota on a Randomized Control Trial, with the initial pilot showing very positive results. Buchanan and Bay have received emails from users telling them that they’ve used Sanvello to help reduce stress related to school and relationships to curing insomnia. Users who may not have received help otherwise have been able to find a new sense of purpose.

By far one of our favorite projects to work on consistently, knowing the results help on a personal level. Especially with younger individuals who may not be able to seek these services due to financial means.
