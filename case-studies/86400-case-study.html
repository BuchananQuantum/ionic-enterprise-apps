:86400 is Australia’s first smartbank, built for smartphones. It is the only digital bank that allows customers to connect accounts from other financial institutions, giving them greater visibility of their financial picture.

What makes :86400 different from other banks?

The biggest difference is that you can open an account without ever having to visit a physical branch. You can do everything online or on your mobile phone – from opening an account to depositing money to transferring funds between accounts.

Another big advantage of :86400 is that there are no fees for using your debit card overseas or for making withdrawals at an ATM abroad – something that often catches people out with traditional banks. And if you need help, our customer service team is available 24/7 via phone or live chat.

Here are three reasons they're different:

  1. Convenience: With no branches, it’s super easy to open an account online or on your mobile phone – wherever you are in the world! And because everything can be done remotely, there’s no need waste time queuing up at a bank branch.
  2. Fees: There are zero fees for using your debit card overseas or withdrawing cash from ATMs abroad – which can save you a lot of money over time!
  3. Customer service: Our customer service team is available 24/7 via phone or live chat if you need help with anything banking-related

The Strategy

As a business owner, you know that it’s important to make your product available to as many people as possible. With a hybrid app development approach, you can build an app that works on both iOS and Android devices using only one codebase. This means less development resources are needed, and the UI will be consistent across all platforms.

If you’re like most people, you probably think of hybrid apps as a second-class citizen. You may have even heard that they perform poorly and are difficult to develop. But the truth is that hybrid apps have come a long way in recent years. Today, they offer many of the same benefits as native apps – and sometimes even more.

For starters, hybrid apps are built with web technologies like HTML5, CSS3 and JavaScript. This makes them easy to develop and deploy because you don’t need separate teams for each platform (iOS, Android etc.). You can use the same codebase for all platforms, which saves time and money. And since browsers have become more powerful over the past few years, programming support for hybrids has grown massively. So today’s hybrids look and feel just like most native apps – often better than some of them!

Another big advantage of hybrids is that they can be deployed through standard app stores like Apple App Store or Google Play Store without having to go through an approval process. This makes it much easier to get your app in front of users – no need to build a website or deal with other distribution channels. In fact, according to recent estimates, hybrids account for roughly 30% of all iOS App Store downloads and nearly 50% of all Google Play Store downloads. So, if you want your app reach as many users as possible quickly Hybrid hasn't always been the best option but now it's something worth considering!
